Scrum Crash Course

(Or how to understand developers in 20 mins.)

Warning: Slides may contain management-buzzwords.

What is Scrum ?

  1. Agile Process

    Being able to respond to change is the key element.

  2. Making the complex simple

    Small steps towards the major goal, instead of large, clunky plans with no room for innovation.

  3. Created for software development

    But usable for anything else - Even planning your holiday.

Why use Scrum?

Business

  1. Faster time to market for most valuable features

  2. Business value sets priorities

Developers

  1. Structured workflow

  2. More visibility of work done - And the consequences of last-minute changes

  3. Developers focus on what they do best, lets business handle priorities

Product

  1. Constant evaluation of featureset, to improve the product

Roles

Product Owner

  1. Maintains the product backlog
  2. Has the final word in regards to the priority of features
  3. Negotiates with interests in the business
  4. Negotiates amounts of features with the team

Scrum Master

  1. Ensures the process is on track
  2. Removes obstacles for the team
  3. Shields the team from outside interference

Team

  1. 5-9 members, crossfunctional
  2. Selects the sprint goal and the end result
  3. Self-organizing

Ceremonies and meetings

Sprint planning

  1. Planning the next scope of next sprint
  2. The team, Scrum Master and Product Owner attends
  3. Potential features are selected
  4. Issues are selected from the top of the backlog, based on team velocity and availability

Daily Scrum

  1. Daily synchronization of work
  2. The meeting is held standing up
  3. Only the team, the Product Owner and the Scrum Master are allowed to speak
    The 3 questions
  1. What did I do yesterday ?
  2. What am I going to do today ?
  3. What obstacles are in my way ?

Ceremonies and meetings

Sprint Review

  1. Presenting the achievements of the sprint
  2. Everyone with an interest in the product is invited
  3. The new features are inspected, and new ideas are welcome
  4. The sprint closes down.

Retrospective

  1. Identify things that work, or can be improved in the process.
  2. The team, Scrum Master and Product Owner attends
    The 3 Post-It's
  1. What went well, and should continue ?
  2. What should be stopped ?
  3. What aren't we doing, that we need to start doing ?
